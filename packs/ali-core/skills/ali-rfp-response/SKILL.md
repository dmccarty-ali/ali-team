# RFP Response Methodology

RFP response best practices based on Shipley Method and APMP standards.

## When I Speak Up

**PLANNING:** Bid/no-bid decisions, proposal structure, compliance strategy
**IMPLEMENTATION:** Writing sections, building compliance matrix, win themes
**GUIDANCE:** Color team reviews, section templates, industry frameworks
**REVIEW:** Compliance validation, win theme integration, scoring simulation

## Key Principles

1. **Customer-centric, not feature-focused** - Answer "why us?" from their perspective
2. **Compliance is non-negotiable** - Missing one requirement = potential disqualification
3. **Executive summary written LAST** - After all sections complete
4. **Win themes throughout** - 25 words or less, woven into every section
5. **Color team reviews are quality gates** - Blue → Pink → Red → Green → Gold → White
6. **Lowest price wins only 10%** - Focus on value, not just cost

## Bid/No-Bid Decision Framework

**Go Criteria:**
- Strong competitive advantage identifiable
- Customer needs align with capabilities
- Win probability supports pursuit
- Resources available
- Deal value justifies investment

**No-Bid Criteria:**
- Competitor advantage too strong
- Significant capability gaps
- Resource constraints
- Poor win probability
- Misalignment with business strategy

## Color Team Review Process

| Team | Focus | Outcome |
|------|-------|---------|
| Blue | Go/no-go strategy, win themes, approach | Bid/no-bid decision |
| Pink | Narrative flow, content organization | Strategic coherence |
| Red | Compliance, persuasiveness (evaluator view) | Scoring simulation |
| Green | Cost realism, pricing logic | Budget credibility |
| Gold | Executive sign-off, messaging | Final approval |
| White | Grammar, formatting, consistency | Production quality |

## Compliance Matrix Structure

**Required columns:**
- RFP requirement ID and reference
- Detailed requirement description
- Corresponding proposal section(s)
- Compliance status (full/partial/gap)
- Responsibility owner

**Key principle:** 100% coverage mandatory.

## Win Theme Development

- Single-sentence strategic summaries (25 words or less)
- Customer-centric, not feature-focused
- Must be unique to your company
- Woven throughout entire proposal

**Formula:** [Customer need] + [Our unique capability] + [Measurable benefit]

## Proposal Section Templates

### Executive Summary
- Written LAST (not first)
- Answers "why us?" not feature summary
- Structure: Customer needs + solution overview + key benefits + ROI

### Technical Approach
- Map to customer pain points
- Address RFP requirements in stated order
- Explain HOW you solve the problem

### Management Approach
- Team structure and expertise
- Project governance
- Risk mitigation strategies

### Past Performance
- Relevant previous projects (70%+ alignment)
- Quantifiable outcomes and metrics

### Pricing
- Value-for-money narrative
- Note: Lowest price wins only 10% of RFPs

## Common Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Missing required documents | Compliance matrix with document checklist |
| Feature-focused language | Rewrite from customer perspective |
| No compliance matrix | Create before writing sections |
| Executive summary first | Write LAST after all sections |

## Key Metrics

- Compliance coverage: 100%
- All color teams engaged
- Win themes in all major sections
- Past performance 70%+ alignment
- Timeline buffer: 48-72 hours for reviews

## Industry Verticals

### Manufacturing / Industrial Sector

**RFP Conventions:**
- Heavy emphasis on implementation methodology and change management
- Expect detailed resource plans with onshore/offshore mix
- Plant/site rollout sequencing is critical
- Integration with existing MES/ERP systems required
- Downtime minimization is key evaluation criterion
- Safety and compliance documentation expected

**Win Themes for Manufacturing:**
- Proven experience with similar plant environments
- Minimal production disruption approach
- Change management expertise for shop floor adoption
- Integration depth with existing systems

## Technical Domains

### Data & Analytics Platforms

**Medallion Architecture:**
- Bronze/Silver/Gold layer positioning
- Data quality progression narrative
- Governance integration at each layer

**Key Technologies:**
- **Iceberg:** Open table format, time travel, schema evolution
- **Starburst:** Federation, query acceleration, data mesh enablement
- **Power BI:** Semantic models, enterprise distribution, governance

**Positioning Strategy:**
- Lead with business outcomes, not technology
- Reference architecture diagrams expected
- Include data governance integration story
- Address existing BI tool migration path

### Data Governance

**Collibra:**
- Data catalog and lineage
- Business glossary
- Policy management
- Stewardship workflows

**SAP MDG (Master Data Governance):**

- Integration with S/4HANA

**Positioning Strategy:**
- Connect governance to regulatory compliance
- Quantify data quality improvement ROI
- Address organizational change management

### ERP Ecosystems

**SAP:**

- Module expertise (FI/CO, MM, SD, PP, etc.)
- Integration with non-SAP systems

**IFS:**
- Asset-intensive industry focus

**Multi-ERP Environments:**
- Integration and data harmonization approach

## Pricing Models

### T&M with DNE (Do Not Exceed)

**Structure:**
- Hourly/daily rates by role
- DNE ceiling for budget protection
- True-up provisions
- Change order process

**Positioning:**
- Flexibility for evolving requirements
- Client maintains cost control via DNE
- Transparency in effort tracking
- Risk sharing between parties

**What to Include:**
- Rate card by role/level
- DNE amount and calculation basis
- Assumptions that affect DNE
- Process for scope changes

### Fixed Price

**When to Use:**
- Well-defined scope with clear deliverables
- Client requires budget certainty
- Competitive differentiation opportunity

**Risk Mitigation:**
- Detailed assumptions section
- Clear change order process
- Milestone-based payments
- Acceptance criteria defined upfront

### Resource Mix Positioning

**Onshore/Offshore:**
- Lead roles onshore (architects, leads, client-facing)
- Delivery capacity offshore
- Overlap hours for collaboration
- Knowledge transfer approach

**Rate Optimization:**
- Justify mix based on task requirements
- Show total cost advantage
- Address quality and communication concerns
- Include escalation and oversight model

## Multi-Workstream Coordination

**When Applicable:**
- Related but separate RFPs from same client
- Multiple evaluation tracks with shared themes
- Cross-functional initiatives requiring unified narrative

**Coordination Patterns:**
- Shared executive summary themes
- Consistent pricing methodology across workstreams
- Cross-referenced past performance
- Unified team structure with workstream leads
- Shared risk and assumption sections

**Pitfalls to Avoid:**
- Inconsistent messaging between proposals
- Conflicting resource commitments
- Different pricing models without justification
- Disconnected governance approaches

**Coordination Checklist:**
- [ ] Win themes aligned across all proposals
- [ ] Resource availability validated across workstreams
- [ ] Pricing methodology consistent (or differences justified)
- [ ] Cross-references accurate and current
- [ ] Timeline dependencies identified
- [ ] Single point of contact for client questions
